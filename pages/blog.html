How Has Technology And The Internet Affected Us In Our Daily Lives

Author Anwesh Dahal

Image by StartupStockPhotos from Pixabay

Technology is the set of tools, gadgets, and techniques that are developed upon our knowledge and understanding of the world. These tools help us to make our work easier and more efficient. Perhaps one of the most important forms of technology in today’s world is the Internet. Internet is a decentralized connection of millions of systems that are spread throughout the world and provide information and services to its users on a massive scale; therefore we can say that it is a network of networks. The Internet has become a staple of modern lifestyle; most of our daily activities are in some way related to the internet and the services it provides, either directly or indirectly. Internet’s sole purpose is to provide the user with the information they require, this information can be directions to their work, the music they want to listen to, or the research materials they want to look at.

The Internet has provided a huge platform for talent and knowledge to grow upon. However, the positive aspects of the internet aren’t limited to its immense contribution to education. Communication is something that has had an even bigger impact of internet technology. People use the internet to communicate all the time because this form of communication is faster, cheaper, and more versatile. Emails have almost replaced snail mails, even though people use Internet Relay Chat (IRC) more than they use emails. Emails are more preferable in a formal work or educational environment. People can conduct conferences and meetings with their peers across the globe without leaving their room, this is called video conference which is also informally referred to as video chat. The internet has also provided an alternative for voice calls; this enables the user to make calls to our peers cheaper.

The Internet has helped a lot of people to flourish their businesses too. As e-commerce is growing rapidly and becoming more preferable among the people. Small business owners get a chance to sell their products and services to a larger number of customers.

The most important contribution made by the internet is to education and research. Students and researchers can get access to this immense resource of information and study materials on the click of a button. Studying through the internet doesn’t just save money that would be spent on buying books or library membership but also provides the most up to date and relevant content. In a world where technology develops at an extremely fast rate, using outdated information isn’t beneficial or rewarding. On the other hand, people don’t have to spend a big sum of money to learn something new. There are a lot of people who post instructional videos on the internet that teach the same thing at a much cheaper rate or sometimes even for free also people can schedule their learning time according to their existing time table. This makes online tutorials more preferable for students and busy people.

Just like everything the internet also has some negative aspects. Since the Internet provides some level of anonymity to its users, so some people use this anonymity with malicious intent. Harassment and cyberbullying have become a common problem for a lot of people on the internet. People tend to harass and bully the people whom they don’t agree with or the people they don’t like. In addition to this, some people use the internet to spread false news and misinformation, just to promote their ideas and views either political or personal. This news easily affects the people who aren’t informed or educated enough on those topics and get influenced by them.

Another major harmful activity conducted on the internet is phishing. Phishing.org defines phishing as, a cybercrime where a target is approached by the attacker disguised in the form of a legitimate and trustworthy figure to obtain their sensitive information like identity, and banking or credit card information. This information is then used to cause harm to the victim in the form of identity theft or financial loss.

The internet is also used to spread computer viruses or malware. According to Norton, a computer virus is a malicious program that can replicate itself much like a real virus and spread from hosts (system) to hosts and harm them. A computer virus is the biggest threat faced by users of the internet. The sole purpose of a computer virus is to cause harm to the victim. Along with this, there are other forms of malicious programs that are spread via the internet like spyware or ransomware.

The Internet can also get addictive since the internet is filled with entertaining resources and contents people may spend hours surfing the web and care less about their personal health, hygiene and the people close to them.

However, the effects that the Internet will have on a person’s life depend upon the way he/she uses the internet. Misusing anything will certainly cause some harm and this applies to the internet as well. In my point of view, the internet is beneficial as long as it’s used ethically. I use the internet for a wide range of activities like learning a new language, to learn programming, streaming movies, reading articles or just know what the weather will be like that day. I believe that if the users are careful and cautious while using the internet they will experience little to no negative aspects of the internet. The internet provides a lot of facilities that when used responsibly can benefit the user in lots of ways.

In conclusion, the internet has both good and bad sides. It has connected people regardless of the place they live in. People get to know the thing that is happening in the world at the click of a button. Our lives have become easier because if we desire we can do a wide range of activities without leaving our homes. Art and culture can be promoted easily. Students don’t have to depend upon their classes to learn new things. However, we can also see an increase in harassment, illegal and bad activities that agitate not only the victim but the society as well. Finally, the experience we get using internet technology or the effects it will have on our lives will depend entirely on the way we used it.
