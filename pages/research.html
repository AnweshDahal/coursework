Preface

Making this website wasn't easy I had to do intensive research to make this website. Every thing from the over theme to a simple card that displays an email were inspired from various website. I gave them my own touch ofcourse, but nevertheless this website wasnt possible without research. Not only I was able to make this website for my course work but I saw some great functionalities and features that immensly improve the quality of a product.

1.1 Traversy Media

The Header

Traversy Media
My Header

Brad Traversy is an online tutor who makes tutorial videos on different web technologies and programming. I too have been following him for a long time, so I went through his website for inspiration. I liked his header as it was simple and intuitive. On the other hand, it matched my website theme as well. In contrast to his website, my website uses the current web page's name rather than the website's logo. and the links are more spread apart.

1.2 Traversy Media

The Email Card

Traversy Media
My Email Link

the contact section on the Traversy Media's website caught my eye too . I liked the way he presented his email in a card which looks like a visiting card. This felt as if he has incorporated a real world element into his website, because of which I decided to do something similar in my website too, but insted of giving just my email. I used this to present an alternative to the contact form used in my website. Unlike Brad Traversy's website my email card does not stand out from the background. However it does contain a @ symbol because its common to use this symbol to symbolize email address and contacts

2. <Yasio/>

Banner Content

<Yasio/> Portfolio
My Banner and Welcome Message

I came across Jan Baszczok's website while I was researching for this course work. the name of his website is YASI0. The thing that intrigued me the most was his introduction. It was concise and introduced someone who is in IT. on the other hand it had an animation that gave off his skills and abilities. I incorporated this feature in my website by introducing myself and adding a javascript that would change the content under the "I am a" section. The background of the banner has a striped pattern and instead of the animation used in the referenced website the text change without an animation

3. The Conversation

Footer

The Conversation Media
My Footer

Instead of a footer with lots of content that recur in all of the webpages. I decided to style my footer according to the style of the footer in The Conversation Media's website. Most of the contents of the footer like social media and other navigation links are integrated into the main content or the header. thus it was a reasonable choice to make the footer as simple as the footer of the referenced website

4. Harrisburg Web Design & Squarespace Developer
My Website Color and Theme

The Harrisburg Web Design & Squarespace Developer belongs to Devon Stank, He is a web developer. His website was the main inspiration for the theme, color scheme, and fonts used in my website. I wasn't going to use just black and white on my website. it was going to contain lots of color like red and blue, but after visiting Devon Stank's website I came to realize that using a lot of designs and color distracts the visitor. That is why I decided to keep my website as minimal as possible with a simple color pallet.

5. GIZMODO

Blog Title and Layout

Gizmodo
My Blog Title

Gizmodo is a website that contains articles about different stuff related to technology and science. I was reading some articles on this website and got an inspiration for styling my blog. The style used u=in the heading is particularly based on the style used in Gizmodo's articles. on the other hand, the share buttons are on the bottom and the article is contained inside a container which is then aligned to the center.

6. Google

Search Bar style

Google Home Page
My Home Page

The button styling on the home page was inspired by the the search bar on Google's Homepage. this is a simple style that adds some uniqueness to the webpage. the slight hover effect achieved by applying shadow , to achieve this I applied a lighter shadow to the buttons initially and when the mouse hovers over it the offset and the the spread of the shadow increases to give an illusion of the button being lifted and the shadow gets darker, to make everything smooth transition was used. the gradient of color from the top left to the bottom right was used to simulate lighting shone from the top right
